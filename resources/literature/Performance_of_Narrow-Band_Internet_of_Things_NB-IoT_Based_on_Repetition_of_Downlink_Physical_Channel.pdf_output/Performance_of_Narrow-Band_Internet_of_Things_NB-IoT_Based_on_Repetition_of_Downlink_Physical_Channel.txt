Performance of Narrow-Band Internet of Things

(NB-IoT) Based on Repetition of Downlink Physical
Channel

Nurul Aizati Ahmad, and Nur Idora Abdul Razak
Universiti Teknologi MARA, 40450 Shah Alam, Selangor MALAYSIA
zatty.ahmad@gmail.com, nuridora@salam.uitm.edu.my

Abstract- Narrow-Band Internet of Things (NB-IoT) offers
low power and wide coverage for IoT services which reuse
existing Long Term Evolution (LTE) technology. Coverage
enhancement at weak signal area may be achieved by channel
repetition to improve signal quality. In this paper, the
performance of NB-IoT is evaluated for three different modes
of deployments by repeating the downlink physical channel.
It is proved that the increase in the number of repetitions will
give better performance of NB-IoT system. It also shows that
the standalone and guard band modes perform better
compared to in-band mode.

Index Terms- NB-IoT, Repetition, In-band, Guardband,
Standalone, NPDSCH

I. INTRODUCTION

NB-IoT is a radio access technology that is defined from the
existing Long-Term Evolution (LTE) technology by 3GPP in
middle of 2016 [4] [12]. The idea of NB-IoT introduction is to
fulfill the demand of markets related to the IoT needs. The main
objectives of this technology are to be able to effectively handle
huge number of connected devices, to lengthen the battery
operated by lowering the power consumptions and improve the
weak and cell edge coverage by signal repetition in physical layer
[1]. Deployment of NB-IoT are based on the guideline that have
been defined in 3GPP Release-13.

NB-IoT supports repetition of the signals in order to boost the
signal power for low powered devices that are in extreme
coverage condition [9]. In [15], number of repetitions depends on
the coverage enhancement level required by a User Equipment
(UE). Repetition can exist in physical downlink channel and
physical uplink channel.

NB-IoT can be deployed in three different modes — inband,
guardband and standalone [1] [6]. Fig. | shows the inband mode
where NB-IoT is deployed within the LTE carrier. NB-loT
requires only one physical resource block (PRB) from LTE
legacy. PRB can be fully utilized by LTE whenever there is no
NB-IoT traffic present. This is due to the integration of structure

and spectrum usage defined in LTE and NB-IoT. To support full
flexible design, two modes of inband operation are defined. The
first mode is named Same-PCI mode where the NB-IoT carrier
has identical parameters, which are the physical cell ID (PCI) and
number of transmit antennas as the LTE. The other mode is
defined as Different-PCI where it considers options of having
different cell ID and number of transmit antennas [6].

these carriers basically an advantage as it can accelerate its

Since NB-IoT is part of LTE family, therefore there are few
common characteristics of NB-IoT and LTE such as the length of
radio frame and the PRB. In NB-IoT, the system bandwidth is
fixed to 180 kHz spectrum which is equivalent to one PRB. Thus,
the number of resource block in NB-IoT system bandwidth is
always one [7] with 12 subcarriers separated by 15 kHz guard
band. Fig. 4 shows the even and odd downlink frames Each frame
has a duration of 10 ms and consists of 10 subframes.

In 3GPP Rel-13, there are new channels and signals are
derived based on LTE channels with some modification to fit into
the narrow bandwidth used by downlink (DL) and uplink (UL) in
NB-IoT. Narrow-band Primary Synchronization Signal (NPSS)
and Narrow-band Secondary Synchronization Signal (NSSS)
provide information on time and frequency synchronization.
NPSS is transmitted in subframe 5 in all radio frames while NSSS
is transmitted in subframe 9 in even radio frames only. Cell
reference is identified by the Narrow-band Reference Signal
(NRS) cell.

Three new downlink channels are Narrow-band Physical
Broadcast Channel (NPBCH), Narrow-band Physical Downlink
Shared Channel (NPDSCH) and Narrow-band Downlink Control
Channel (NPDCCH). NPBCH carries the Master Information
Block (MIB) and occupies subframe 0 in every frame. NPDCCH
carries downlink control information. NPDSCH carries the data
intended for the specific user, in addition to paging, system
information and random access response.

In NB-IoT network, when User Equipment (UE) is an
ideal mode it will periodically monitors NPDCCH to check
whether there is any paging message which indicates the presence
of downlink traffic for the UE. Once the UE recognized the
presence of traffic for the UE, it will decode NPDSCH to identify
the contents of the traffic. Therefore, successful decoding of
NPDSCH is a crucial factor in providing a reliable NB-loT
services [6]. Thus, when the system enables the NPDSCH
repetition transmission, it will increase the number of received
signal detected and decoded at the weak signal area or cell edge.
NB-IoT adjust the number of transmission repetition based on the
signal strength and increase the number of transmission repetition
to ensure effective data transmission at coverage edges.

The NB-IoT signaling protocol uses new messages - Master
Information Block NB (MIB-NB) and System Information Block
Type I-NB (SIB1-NB). These two messages are different from the
SIB and MIB messages in LTE standard in terms of the content
of the message, the scheduling type and the physical channel.
Even though these two messages are different from LTE standard,
the eNodeB still can manage these new messages in a same
manner by differentiating the UE types and messages [4]. MIB in
legacy LTE only contains high level information, but in MIB-NB
it contains more details information to acquire SIB1-NB including
the operation mode of NB-IoT. In [13], the specification of SIB1-
NB messages is explained in detail. SIBI-NB message is
transmitted every 2560 ms which is equivalent to 256 frames.
Within this period, this message can be repeated for several times.
In NB-IoT standard, UE shall receive minimum one MIB-NB
message and one SIBI-NB message before Random Access
Channel (RACH) process and connection establishment with
eNodeB.

Il. SYSTEM MODEL

In order to ideally show the effect of repetition, the controlled
channel performance has been considered. The performance of
NPDSCH repetition will be presented by Block Error Rate
(BLER) simulation under Addictive White Gaussian Noise
(AWGN). The improvement of signal quality is expected to be
illustrated when number of NPDSCH repetition are varies at three
different modes of NB-IoT deployment — inband, guardband and
standalone (non-LTE carrier). To have a comparable result, there
are few constant variables configured in all three modes of NB-
IoT deployment.

In [15] the number of NPDSCH repetitions and the transport
block size (TBS) are affected depending on the NPDSCH channel
whether it is carrying SIBI-NB or not. In this simulation,
NPDSCH is configured to carry SIB1-NB. The number of
repetitions depends on the TBS tables in [15].

The transport blocks of size 680 undergo 24-bit CRC
encoding, convolutional encoding and rate matching stages in
order to obtain the NPDSCH bits which are repeated according to
repetition configured. Scrambling, QPSK modulation, layer
mapping and precoding are applied to form complex NPDSCH
symbols. AWGN is added to these NPDSCH symbols and then
channel decoding and demodulation are performed to recover the
transport block. After de-scrambling, the repetitive subframes are
soft-combined before rate recover. The transport block error rate
(BLER) of decoded blocks are calculated.

Ill. | SIMULATION RESULTS

To present the effect of repetition, the channels are simulated
as perfect channels where there is no channel propagation
considered during simulation. Fig. 5 represents the effect of
repetition transmission in inband mode. As NPDSCH channels
are carrying SIB1-NB massage, thus the number of repetitions can
only be repeat either 4, 8 or 16 as defined in 3GPP standard. It
shows for BLER 107, the SNR at 4, 8 and 16 repetitions are -3.1
dB, -6.2 dB and -9.4 dB respectively. It clearly shows that by
having repetition transmission, more signals can be detected
correctly at low SNR as the number of repetitions is increasing. It
shows that around 3 dB improvement can be achieved if the
number of repetitions is doubled.

TV. | CONCLUSION

Based on the result achieved, it can be concluded that
repetition of NPDSCH improved signals quality at the lower SNR
where more signals are detected and decoded correctly. As the
number of NPDSCH channels repetition are increased the signal
able to reach even at the lower SNR. Hence, it will enhance
coverage at the weak area or cell edge. At lower SNR, more
signals are detected and decoded in both guardband and
standalone modes compared to inband mode. Regardless any
modes of deployments, repetition transmission is possible to boost
the coverage at lower SNR. Coverage improvement is important
as NB-loT users can have better experience in applying NB-IoT.
